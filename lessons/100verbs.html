Top One Hundred English Verbs

A

Agree: I agree with my friend.

Argue: They argue every night.

Arrive: What time does your plane arrive?

Ask: He asks me a question.

B

Be: She is afraid when she hears that sound.

Become: One day, I will become a famous actor.

Begin: He begins his work early in the morning.

Believe: Do you believe in ghosts?

Break: He usually breaks his cellphone cover.

Bring: Please bring your dictionary to the next lesson.

Build: They're building some new houses next to the lake.

Buy: I want to buy a new coat.

c

Call: Can you call me tomorrow?

Carry: Can you help me carry this suitcase?

Catch: I didn't catch what you said.

Check: The teacher checks my homework everyday.

Choose: I want to choose my favorite major.

Clean: It takes an hour to clean the car.

Come: Where do you come from?

Cook: She cooks every day for her family.

Cost: We need to cut our advertising costs.

d

Die: Each year, thousands of people die of hunger.

Do: I do my homework before school.

Drink: She drinks a cup of coffee, then goes out.

Drive: You drive too fast!

e

Eat: Do you eat meat?

f

Fall: He falls off his bike and hurts his knee.

Feel: I feel cold.

Fight: Those two children fight like cats and dogs.

Find: Can you find a cure for the disease?

Finish: I'll finish my homework after dinner.

forget: She always forgets to bring her homework.

g

Get: Can you get me some apples when you're at the shops?

Give: He gives me a great book for my birthday.

Go: They go to the beach every summer.

h

Hate: I hate getting ill.

Have: Do you have enough money with you?

Hear: I hear so many strange sounds in this mountain.

Help: Can you help me?

Hold: Please hold the line.

k

Know: Do you know how to get to their office?

l

Learn: You need to learn all these dates before the test.

Leave: I leave home at 8 a.m. every morning.

Like: Do you like sports?

Listen: Please listen to me.

Live: Where do they live?

Look: Look at this picture!

Lose: They always lose the match.

Love: I love her music.

m

Make: She makes a card for her mother.

Mean: What does this word mean?

Meet: Let's meet at the cafe.

n

Need: You'll need to bring your passport with you.

o

Order: Are you ready to order yet?

Organise: I'd like to organise a party for my birthday.

p

Pay: Where can I pay?

Phone: Can you phone me tomorrow?

Play: They like playing football in the park.

Post: I need to post this letter.

Put: Where do you put the TV remote?

Prepare: She prepares her lessons every evening.

r

Read: What sort of books do you read?

Relax: She relaxes by watching TV.

Remember: Do you remember the time of the appointment?

Repair: Can you repair this bike for me?

Return: What day do they return from holiday?

Ride: The kids ride their bikes every day.

Run: We have to run for the bus.

s

Save: It's a good idea to save some money every month.

Say: What do you say?

See: Can you see the sea from your house?

Sell: They sell computer harware there.

Send: He sends her some flowers for her birthday.

Share: Please share the book.

Show: She shows me her new painting.

Sit: Please sit down.

Sleep: My baby usually goes to sleep after a feed.

speak: I'd like to speak to the manager, please.

Spend: We spend a lot of money on our meals.

Stay: Where do you stay in Tehran?

Start: She will start school in a year.

Stop: The train stops at every station.

Study: We study hard before the exam.

t

Take: Can you take the rubbish out, please?

Talk: They always talk in class.

Teach: She teaches Maths and Physics.

Tell: The teacher tells the students to study for the exam.

Think: What do you think?

Travel: He travells around the world.

Try: He tries hard at school.

Turn: Please turn off the TV when the programme finishes.

u

Understand: I don't understand.

Use: What type of toothpaste do you use?

w

Wait: We wait an hour for the train.

Wake up: I wake up early in the morning.

Walk: They walk to work.

Want: I don't want to move house this year.

Watch: She watches TV in the evening.

Wear: He always wears jeans at the weekend.

Win: Do you think you will win the match?

Work: They work hard.
