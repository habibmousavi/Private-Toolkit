Roses


A man stops at a flower shop to order some flowers to be wired to his mother who lives two hundred miles away. As he gets out of his car he notices a young girl sitting on the curb sobbing. He asks her what is wrong and she replies, “I want to buy a red rose for my mother. But I only have seventy-five cents, and a rose costs two dollars.”

The man smiles and says, “Come on in with me. I’ll buy you a rose.” He buys the little girl her rose and orders his own mother’s flowers. As they are leaving he offers the girl a ride home. She says, “Yes, please! You can take me to my mother.” She directs him to a cemetery, where she places the rose on a grave.

The man returns to the flower shop, cancels the wire order, picks up a bouquet and drives the two hundred miles to his mother’s house.

Moral: Life is Short. Spend much time as you can loving and caring people who love you. Enjoy each moment with them before it’s too late. There is nothing more important than family.
