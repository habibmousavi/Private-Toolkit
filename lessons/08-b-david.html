David and the door


Once, when David the fool is a boy, his mother goes out for a picnic. Before she goes out, she says to him, “David, while I am away, stay near the door and watch it all the time.” She says this because there are a lot of thieves in their town.

David sits down beside the door. After an hour one of his uncles comes. He says to David, “Where is your mother? ”

“At a picnic,” he answers.

“Well,” says the uncle, “we want to come to your house this evening. Go and tell her.”

His uncle then goes away and David begins to think.

“Mother says, ‘Watch the door all the time!’ and uncle says, ‘go and tell her’!”

David thinks for a long time and at last, pulls the door down, puts it on his back and goes to his mother with it.
